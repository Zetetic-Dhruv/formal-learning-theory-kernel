\chapter{The Measurability Layer}\label{chap:measurability}

The fundamental theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:vc-char}) was stated in Chapter~\ref{chap:pac} under
a null-measurability hypothesis on the uniform convergence bad event.
This chapter opens that hypothesis. The structure of the chapter is a
three-step argument. Section~\ref{sec:borel-param} introduces the
Borel-parameterized setting and identifies the precise regularity the
symmetrization step requires. Section~\ref{sec:choquet-bridge} shows
that in the Borel-parameterized setting the ghost-gap bad event is
automatically analytic, and that analytic sets are null-measurable via
the Choquet capacitability theorem. Section~\ref{sec:borel-analytic-sep}
exhibits a concept class whose bad event is analytic but not Borel,
certifying that the measurability refinement of the fundamental
theorem is strict.

This chapter contains the one piece of mathematical content in the
blueprint that is net-new to the formalization effort. The textbook
that supplies the prose for Chapters~\ref{chap:primitives}
through~\ref{chap:separations} does not discuss the Borel-analytic
separation, because the separation was discovered during the Lean
formalization in the process of auditing which regularity hypotheses
the symmetrization step actually uses.

\section{Borel parameterization and the symmetrization route}\label{sec:borel-param}

The symmetrization argument at the heart of the uniform convergence
proof rewrites the one-sided bad event as a two-sided ghost-gap event.
Given a sample $S$ of size $m$, a ghost sample $S'$ of the same size,
and a gap threshold $\eps$, the ghost-gap bad event is
\[
  \mathrm{Bad}_2(C, m, \eps)
    = \left\{ (S, S') \in X^m \times X^m :
      \sup_{h \in C} \bigl| \emprisk_{S'}(h) - \emprisk_S(h) \bigr|
      > \eps \right\}.
\]
The probability bound on $\mathrm{Bad}_2$ hinges on applying Hoeffding's
inequality to each of the growth-function-many effective labelings of
the combined sample and then controlling the supremum via a union
bound. For this bound to apply, $\mathrm{Bad}_2$ must be measurable
with respect to every product measure $D^{\otimes 2m}$ on
$X^m \times X^m$ built from a distribution $D$ on $X$.

The regularity the argument actually uses is \emph{null-measurability}
of $\mathrm{Bad}_2$ with respect to the completion of the product
measure, not Borel measurability with respect to the product Borel
structure. This is a real difference. There exist sets in the
completion that are not Borel, and we will construct one in
Section~\ref{sec:borel-analytic-sep}. The weaker requirement is all the
proof needs, and using it gives a strictly more general theorem.

\begin{defn}[WellBehavedVCMeasTarget]
\label{def:wbvc-meas-target}
  \lean{WellBehavedVCMeasTarget}
  \leanok
A concept class $C$ over a measurable space $(X, \Sigma)$ satisfies
\emph{WellBehavedVCMeasTarget} if for every sample size $m$, every
gap threshold $\eps$, and every probability distribution $D$ on $X$,
the two-sided ghost-gap bad event $\mathrm{Bad}_2(C, m, \eps)$ is a
null-measurable subset of $(X^m \times X^m, \Sigma^{\otimes 2m})$ with
respect to the product measure $D^{\otimes 2m}$. Null-measurability
means that $\mathrm{Bad}_2$ sits in the completion of the product
$\sigma$-algebra.
\end{defn}

\begin{defn}[KrappWirth well-behaved]
\label{def:krapp-wirth}
  \lean{KrappWirthWellBehaved}
  \leanok
A concept class $C$ satisfies the KrappWirth well-behaved hypothesis
of Krapp and Wirth~\cite{KrappWirth2024} if for every $m$ and $\eps$,
$\mathrm{Bad}_2(C, m, \eps)$ is a Borel subset of $X^m \times X^m$ in
the product Borel structure.
\end{defn}

\begin{prop}[KrappWirth implies WellBehavedVCMeasTarget]
\label{prop:krapp-wirth-implies-wbvc}
  \lean{KrappWirthWellBehaved.toWellBehavedVC}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:wbvc-meas-target,def:krapp-wirth}
Every Borel set is null-measurable with respect to any completion of a
Borel probability measure. Consequently, any concept class carrying
\texttt{KrappWirthWellBehaved} also carries
\texttt{WellBehavedVCMeasTarget}.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
Borel sets lie in the product $\sigma$-algebra by definition, hence in
its completion with respect to any measure. The inclusion
$\mathcal{B} \subseteq \mathcal{B}_\mu^\ast$ (Borel into completion) is
immediate.
\end{proof}

\begin{defn}[Borel-parameterized concept class]
\label{def:borel-param}
A concept class $C \subseteq \{0,1\}^X$ over a measurable space
$(X, \Sigma)$ is \emph{Borel-parameterized} by a standard Borel space
$(\Theta, \mathcal{B}_\Theta)$ if there exists a jointly measurable
evaluation map $\mathrm{eval} : \Theta \times X \to \{0,1\}$ such that
$C = \{\,\mathrm{eval}(\theta, \cdot) : \theta \in \Theta\,\}$.
\end{defn}

Borel parameterization is the standard regularity assumption in
descriptive-set-theory approaches to learning theory. Every concept
class considered in practice admits a Borel parameterization: finite
classes, neural networks with measurable weights, decision trees of
bounded depth, thresholds, halfspaces, and so on. The kernel's
Borel-parameterized setting captures exactly this regularity.

\begin{defn}[Parameterized ghost-gap bad event]
\label{def:param-bad-event}
  \lean{paramBadEvent}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:borel-param}
For a Borel-parameterized class with evaluation map $\mathrm{eval}$,
sample size $m$, and gap threshold $\eps$, the \emph{parameterized
ghost-gap bad event} is
\[
  \mathrm{Bad}_{\mathrm{eval}}(m, \eps) = \bigcup_{\theta \in \Theta}
  \Bigl\{\, (S, S') :
    \bigl| \emprisk_{S'}(\mathrm{eval}(\theta, \cdot))
    - \emprisk_S(\mathrm{eval}(\theta, \cdot)) \bigr| > \eps
  \,\Bigr\}.
\]
This is the projection of a jointly Borel event in
$\Theta \times X^m \times X^m$ onto the last two factors.
\end{defn}

\section{The analytic measurability bridge}\label{sec:choquet-bridge}

\begin{lemma}[Projections of Borel sets are analytic]
\label{lem:borel-proj-analytic}
The projection of a Borel set in a standard Borel space onto one
factor of a product is an analytic set. This is the defining property
of analytic sets: they are exactly the projections of Borel sets,
equivalently the continuous images of Polish spaces.
\end{lemma}

Applied to Definition~\ref{def:param-bad-event}, the lemma gives that
the parameterized ghost-gap bad event is always analytic in
$X^m \times X^m$. The rest of the measurability argument is to show
that analytic sets are null-measurable with respect to every Borel
probability measure. This is the content of the Choquet capacitability
theorem.

\begin{thm}[Choquet capacitability~{\cite[Theorem 30.13]{Kechris1995}}]
\label{thm:choquet-capacity}
  \lean{MeasureTheory.AnalyticSet.compactCap_eq}
  \leanok
Let $\mu$ be a Borel probability measure on a Polish space. For every
analytic set $A$, the outer measure of $A$ equals the supremum of
$\mu(K)$ over compact sets $K \subseteq A$. Equivalently, $A$ is
$\mu$-capacitable: its outer and inner measures agree, so $A$ is in
the completion of the Borel $\sigma$-algebra with respect to $\mu$.
\end{thm}

Choquet capacitability is a pure descriptive-set-theory result. Its
proof in the kernel is 416 lines and is independent of learning
theory: it belongs to
\texttt{FLT\_Proofs.PureMath.ChoquetCapacity} and is
Mathlib-contributable as a standalone result. The blueprint treats it
as a black-box citation because its proof is too long to reproduce and
too orthogonal to the learning theory story to justify inclusion.

\begin{cor}[Analytic sets are null-measurable]
\label{cor:analytic-null-measurable}
  \lean{analyticSet_nullMeasurableSet}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:choquet-capacity}
Every analytic set in a Polish space is null-measurable with respect
to every Borel probability measure.
\end{cor}

Applied to the parameterized ghost-gap bad event of
Definition~\ref{def:param-bad-event},
Corollary~\ref{cor:analytic-null-measurable} gives the regularity the
symmetrization step needs.

\begin{prop}[Borel-parameterized classes satisfy WellBehavedVCMeasTarget]
\label{prop:borel-param-implies-wbvc}
  \lean{analyticSet_nullMeasurableSet_ghostPairs}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:borel-param,def:param-bad-event,cor:analytic-null-measurable,def:wbvc-meas-target}
Every Borel-parameterized concept class carries
\texttt{WellBehavedVCMeasTarget}. The bad event
$\mathrm{Bad}_{\mathrm{eval}}(m, \eps)$ is analytic by
Lemma~\ref{lem:borel-proj-analytic}, hence null-measurable by
Corollary~\ref{cor:analytic-null-measurable}, hence in the completion
of the product $\sigma$-algebra.
\end{prop}

The fundamental theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:vc-char}) therefore applies
to every Borel-parameterized concept class, including the classes for
which it was originally stated under the stronger Borel hypothesis of
Krapp and Wirth.

\section{The Borel-analytic separation}\label{sec:borel-analytic-sep}

Section~\ref{sec:choquet-bridge} showed that the null-measurability
hypothesis is sufficient for the fundamental theorem. This section
shows that it is strictly weaker than the Borel hypothesis by
constructing a concept class whose ghost-gap bad event is analytic and
null-measurable but not Borel.

\begin{defn}[Singleton class over a set]
\label{def:singleton-class}
  \lean{singletonClassOn}
  \leanok
For a set $A \subseteq \R$, define the \emph{singleton class over $A$}
as the concept class on $\R$ given by
\[
  C_A = \{\,\mathbf{0}\,\} \cup \{\,\mathbf{1}_{\{a\}} : a \in A\,\}
\]
where $\mathbf{0}$ is the everywhere-false concept and
$\mathbf{1}_{\{a\}}$ is the indicator of the point $a$.
\end{defn}

Every hypothesis in $C_A$ is individually measurable: the zero concept
is measurable because it is constant, and each singleton indicator is
measurable because $\{a\}$ is a Borel set. This is verified as
\lean{singletonClassOn_measurable}. The non-trivial question is
whether the bad event associated to $C_A$ inherits Borel structure.

\begin{defn}[Planar witness event]
\label{def:planar-witness}
  \lean{planarWitnessEvent}
  \leanok
For $A \subseteq \R$, the \emph{planar witness event} is
\[
  W_A = \{\, (x, y) \in \R^2 : y \in A \text{ and } x \neq y \,\}.
\]
\end{defn}

\begin{lemma}[Bad event as preimage of planar witness]
\label{lem:bad-eq-preimage}
  \lean{singleton_badEvent_eq_preimage_planar}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:singleton-class,def:planar-witness}
For the singleton class $C_A$, the one-step bad event
$\mathrm{Bad}(C_A, 1, 1/2)$ equals the preimage of the planar witness
$W_A$ under the sample-pair projection
$(\mathrm{Fin}\,1 \to \R) \times (\mathrm{Fin}\,1 \to \R) \to \R^2$.
\end{lemma}

The lemma reduces a question about a bad event on
$\R^1 \times \R^1$ to a question about a planar set in $\R^2$. The
following two theorems characterize the planar set.

\begin{thm}[Planar witness is analytic]
\label{thm:planar-analytic}
  \lean{planarWitnessEvent_analytic}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:planar-witness}
For every analytic set $A \subseteq \R$, the planar witness $W_A$ is
an analytic subset of $\R^2$.
\end{thm}

\begin{thm}[Planar witness fails to be Borel]
\label{thm:planar-not-borel}
  \lean{planarWitnessEvent_not_measurable}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:planar-witness}
There exists an analytic non-Borel set $A \subseteq \R$ for which the
planar witness $W_A$ is analytic but not Borel. The existence of such
$A$ is classical: any complete analytic set in $\R$ suffices (for
example, a specific projection of the complement of a well-founded
tree), and the projection $A \mapsto W_A$ preserves the non-Borel
property.
\end{thm}

\begin{cor}[Borel-analytic separation]
\label{cor:borel-analytic-separation}
  \lean{singleton_badEvent_not_measurable}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:bad-eq-preimage,thm:planar-analytic,thm:planar-not-borel,prop:borel-param-implies-wbvc,def:krapp-wirth,def:wbvc-meas-target}
There exists a concept class $C$ over $\R$ such that $C$ carries
\texttt{WellBehavedVCMeasTarget} (via the analytic measurability
bridge) but $C$ does not carry \texttt{KrappWirthWellBehaved} (because
its ghost-gap bad event is analytic and not Borel). Consequently, the
fundamental theorem applies to $C$ under the NullMeasurableSet
refinement but not under the Borel hypothesis of Krapp and Wirth.
\end{cor}

The corollary closes the loop opened at the start of this chapter. The
NullMeasurableSet refinement of the fundamental theorem is not
cosmetic: the singleton class $C_A$ over an analytic non-Borel set is
a concrete witness that the Borel hypothesis is strictly stronger than
what the symmetrization proof actually uses. The kernel formalizes
this as a chain of five theorems in
\texttt{FLT\_Proofs.Theorem.BorelAnalyticSeparation}, each one
corresponding to a step in the argument above.
