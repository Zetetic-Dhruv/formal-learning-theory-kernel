## X. The Discovery Hypergraph

A dependency DAG shows what depends on what. It does not show why. Overlay the proof world model (Section VIII) on the module dependency graph and the structure becomes a hypergraph: nodes are modules, edges are import dependencies, and hyperedges are metaprograms -- convex hulls wrapping the modules that participate in each proof method.

The hypergraph makes three things visible that the flat DAG hides.

### The overview

![Discovery Hypergraph: Metaprograms over Module Dependencies](assets/hypergraph_overview.png)

35 module nodes across layers L0-L7. 6 metaprogram hyperedges as shaded convex hulls. ~40 import dependency edges. Layout: Sugiyama framework with barycenter heuristic for within-layer node ordering (Sugiyama, Tagawa, Toda 1981; crossing reduction per Kocurova 2007). Machine-generated by `scripts/generate_hypergraph.py`.

**Observation 1: The hulls do not overlap.** The PAC chain hull (MP1-MP5) occupies layers L3-L6 on the left-center. The Borel-analytic bridge hull (MP17, MP20) occupies L4-L6 on the right. The Online potential hull (MP9-MP11) and Gold locking hull (MP12) are isolated in L3-L6. The PAC-Bayes hull (MP21) sits alone at L5-L6. No hull spans all three classical paradigms. This is the paradigm lock theorem of Section VIII, visible.

**Observation 2: The longest hull spans 4 layers.** The PAC chain wraps modules from VCDimension (L3) through Symmetrization and Generalization (L4) to Concentration and Exchangeability (L5) to Thm.PAC (L6). This 4-layer span is the infrastructure depth that makes the PAC characterization the most expensive proof in the kernel (7,925 lines across the hull). The Online and Gold hulls span 3 layers. The PAC-Bayes hull spans 2. Infrastructure depth correlates with proof complexity.

**Observation 3: Measurability is a hub.** The Measurability node (L4) appears inside three hulls: PAC chain, Borel-analytic bridge, and Separation witness. It is the only module shared across hulls. This is the structural evidence for Section V's claim that measurability is the bridge concept connecting learning theory paradigms to descriptive set theory.

### Dead branches

The hypergraph records what was built. The dead branches record what was tried and killed. Each dead branch is a proof route that was explored, found to be provably blocked, and abandoned. The abandonment is itself a discovery.

| Dead branch | What was discovered | Effect on the kernel |
|------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| NFL for finite X | VCDim(Set.univ) = \|X\| for finite X; memorizer learns Set.univ | All NFL theorems require `[Infinite X]` |
| BranchWise Littlestone | const_true/const_false gives LDim = infinity for a 1-mistake class | `Theorem/Online.lean` defines path-wise `LTree.isShattered` |
| Direct union bound | Produces 2^{2m}, not GrowthFunction(C, 2m) | 56% of the codebase is symmetrization infrastructure |
| UC without regularity | Bad event not measurable for uncountable C | `WellBehavedVC` regularity hypothesis required |
| PAC with existential Dm | Existential Dm depends on target c via memorizer | `Measure.pi` (distribution-free) in PACLearnable definition |

In the hypergraph, each dead branch is a hull that was never built. NFL for finite X would have been a single-module hull at L6; instead, the `[Infinite X]` guard forces the proof through the full PAC chain. Direct union bound would have bypassed Symmetrization entirely; instead, the 3,027-line symmetrization infrastructure exists because the shortcut fails. The dead branches explain the shape of the living hulls.

### The sorry nodes

Two nodes in the hypergraph carry sorry markers. Both sit at the boundary of the kernel:

- `vcdim_finite_imp_compression` (Generalization.lean): forward direction of the compression characterization. Blocked by Moran-Yehudayoff 2016 construction absent from Mathlib.
- `bhmz_middle_branch` (Extended.lean): middle branch of the universal trichotomy. Blocked by BHMZ STOC 2021 construction absent from Mathlib.

Both are in extended theorems (not core). Both are blocked by the same obstacle: a construction that exists in the literature but has not been formalized in Mathlib. The core kernel (PAC, Online, Gold characterizations and all separations) is sorry-free.

### Regeneration

The hypergraph figures are machine-generated and deterministic:

```bash
python3 scripts/generate_hypergraph.py
# Outputs: assets/hypergraph_overview.png
#          assets/hypergraph_pac.png
#          assets/hypergraph_online_gold.png
#          assets/hypergraph_dst.png
```

Layout algorithm: Sugiyama framework with manual layer assignment (L0-L7 from premise architecture) and barycenter heuristic for within-layer crossing reduction (20 alternating sweeps). Hyperedge hulls computed via `scipy.spatial.ConvexHull` with 0.6-unit padding. Dependencies: `matplotlib`, `numpy`, `scipy`.
